Arnab Mondal

Engineering Ph.D. Candidate at the School of Chemical, Materials and Biomedical Engineering, University of Georgia.

I work with Professor Hitesh Handa on nitric oxide (NO)- based translational research, developing NO-producing materials with therapeutic potential. Specifically,  my research is focused on improving the multifunctionality of NO-releasing polymer surfaces often used for medical device coatings to prevent medical device infection or improve hemocompatibility.

About

I was born and raised in Durgapur (West Bengal, India). I attended the West Bengal University of Technology (aka MAKAUT) for my undergraduate degree. I studied biotechnology because of an interest in working at the intersection of engineering and biology. During my master’s research, I worked on environmental engineering to focus on bioremediation of crude oil sludge. However, I transitioned back to biomaterials research during my Ph.D. and successfully applied microbiological techniques learned during my master’s research. My passion remains with developing biomaterials and significantly leverage therapeutic interface design to solve modern-day healthcare problems.
